Title: An Ontological Analysis of Faults
Authors: Y. Kitamura and R. Mizoguchi
Reference:AI Technical Report 99-02, I.S.I.R., Osaka University, March, 1999

Abstract: In an interactive system for problem solving, its transparency concerning capability and limitation is critical for ease of understanding to facilitate the utility of the system. Our aim here is explication of capabilities and limitations of diagnostic systems. We focus on the process in which faults are induced and establish an ontology of fault including several categories of fault. It provides us with a vocabulary for specifying the scope of diagnosis. Firstly, the ontology enables us to specify capability of diagnostic systems for transparency to users. Next, we design an interactive system to enumerate the "deeper causes" of a malfunction. The ontology enables the human users to control the diagnostic process in a plausible-first way interactively
