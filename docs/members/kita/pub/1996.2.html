Title: Domain Ontology Design for Model-based Reasoning and Its Evaluation
Authors: Y. Kitamura, M. Ikeda, R. Mizoguchi
Reference: Technical Report AI-TR-96-2, I.S.I.R., Osaka Univ.

Abstract: This paper discusses a domain ontology of fluid systems for model-based reasoning based on two kernel ontologies of time resolution and causality of components, aiming at explicit description of design rationales of model-based reasoning systems for knowledge reuse. Our ontology design has been done according to the following two criteria: intuitive causal ordering in terms of components and reusability of component models. We discuss firstly units of time resolution necessary for reasoning systems to causal ordering of complex behavior originated in various configurations of components and discuss necessary contents of domain ontologies for the time resolution. Next, a kernel ontology of causality is presented which contributes to capture context-independent causal properties of components and to derive appropriate causal relations. On the basis of these kernel ontologies, causal properties of fluid-related components in fluid systems and general properties of fluid and heat for deriving global constraints have been identified. A method of qualitative reasoning and causal ordering based on the ontologies is discussed together with its capability. The ontologies have been successfully applied to a nuclear power plant modeling and its qualitative simulation. Reasoning results matched those obtained by a domain expert.
